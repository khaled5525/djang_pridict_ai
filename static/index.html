The most eye diseases that we are interested

Non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy (NPDR):

Condition caused by diabetes that weakens blood vessels in the retina. In mild NPDR, weak blood vessels leak fluid or blood, and may bulge. In severe NPDR, vision can be affected by fluid buildup in the macula, requiring treatment.

Proliferative diabetic retinopathy:

Severe stage of diabetic retinopathy where abnormal new blood vessels grow in the eye, causing leakage and pressure increase.

Diabetic Macular Edema (DME):

1-Occurs when fluid builds up in the central part of the retina (macula).

2-Can lead to blurred vision and vision loss.

3-Usually treated with anti-VEGF injections or laser therapy.

Diabetic Retinal Vascular Occlusion (DRVO):

1-Occurs when blood vessels in the retina become blocked.

2-Can lead to vision loss in the affected area.

3-Requires regular monitoring with an eye doctor for evaluation and treatment.
